Glossary of Terms

Terms: Definitions:
Biologics A diverse group of medicines which includes vaccines, growth factors, immune modulators, monoclonal antibodies, as well as products derived from human blood and plasma
Materials The matter an object is made up of.
Small Molecule Low molecular weight organic compound that may regulate a biological process, with a size on the order of 1 nm.
SMILE "Simplified Molecular Input Line Entry System,‚Äù which is used to translate a chemical's three-dimensional structure into a string of symbols that is easily understood by computer software
Ligand An ion or molecule with a functional group that binds to a central metal atom to form a coordination complex.
Protein Polypeptide structures consisting of one or more long chains of amino acid residues.
Scaffold A protein that is used as a starting point for the design of antibody mimetics
Compund A chemical substance composed of many identical molecules containing atoms from more than one chemical element held together by chemical bonds.
Enzyme A protein that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without being changed by the reaction; an organic catalyst.
Hydrophobicity The physical property of a molecule that is seemingly repelled from a mass of water.
